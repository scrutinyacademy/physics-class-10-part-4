Electromagnetism
TS SSC Class 10 · Boards 2026

🔍 Oersted's Discovery & Magnetic Field

  • Hans Christian Oersted (1820): Compass needle deflects when placed under current-carrying wire → electricity & magnetism are related
  • Magnetic Field: Force applied without physical contact; has both strength AND direction
  • Magnetic field lines: Imaginary closed loops; tangent at any point gives the direction of field
  • Crowded field lines = Strong field (near poles); Spread lines = Weak field (far from poles)
  • Uniform field: Both strength AND direction constant throughout
  • Non-uniform field: Strength or direction changes from point to point
  • Magnetic flux (Φ): Number of field lines passing through plane ⊥ to field. SI unit = Weber (Wb)
  • Magnetic flux density B = Φ/A. SI unit = Tesla (T) = Wb/m². Also called magnetic induction
  • General flux formula: Φ = BA cosθ (θ = angle between B and normal to plane)

🔌 Magnetic Field Due to Currents

  • Straight current-carrying wire → Circular concentric field lines (closed loops confirmed!)
  • Right-hand thumb rule: Thumb = current direction; Curled fingers = B direction
  • Current OUT of page (⊙): Field lines ANTICLOCKWISE | Current INTO page (⊗): Field lines CLOCKWISE
  • Circular coil: B perpendicular to plane. Anticlockwise current viewed from front → North pole (field toward you)
  • Right-hand rule for coil: Curl fingers with current → thumb = B direction
  • Solenoid: Long wire wound in close-packed helix. B resembles bar magnet. Has N and S poles
  • Solenoid field: Outside = North to South; Inside = South to North. Field lines are CLOSED LOOPS

⚡ Force on Moving Charges & Wires

  • Force on moving charge: F = qvB sinθ (θ = angle between v and B)
  • F = qvB when v ⊥ B (maximum) | F = 0 when v ∥ B (parallel to field → zero force)
  • Right-hand rule: Fingers → velocity, curl → field, thumb → force (for positive charge)
  • For negative charge: Find force for positive charge, then REVERSE direction
  • Charged particle in ⊥ field → circular path: F acts as centripetal force
  • Radius: r = mv/Bq | Time period: T = 2πm/Bq (independent of velocity!)
  • Force on current wire: F = ILB sinθ (general) = ILB when I ⊥ B
  • Wire moves to WEAKER field region (from high to low field intensity)

🔧 Electric Motor

  • Principle: Current-carrying rectangular coil in uniform B → experiences torque (couple) → rotates
  • AB and CD: Equal and opposite forces → net force = 0 but net TORQUE ≠ 0 → rotation
  • DC Motor: Split ring COMMUTATOR reverses current direction every half rotation → continuous rotation
  • AC Motor uses slip rings instead of commutator
  • Brushes (B₁, B₂) maintain contact with rotating commutator/slip rings
  • Energy conversion: ELECTRICAL → MECHANICAL energy
  • Fleming's Left Hand Rule: Forefinger = B direction, Middle finger = Current direction, Thumb = Force direction

🌊 Electromagnetic Induction (Faraday & Lenz)

  • EMI: Production of induced current due to RELATIVE MOTION between coil and magnetic field (Faraday & Henry)
  • Faraday's Law: Induced EMF (ε) = Rate of change of flux = ΔΦ/Δt
  • For N-turn coil: ε = N(ΔΦ/Δt) where Φ = flux linked with one turn
  • Rapid change in flux → Greater induced EMF. Stationary magnet → NO induced EMF
  • Lenz's Law: Induced current opposes the CHANGE IN FLUX that caused it (based on conservation of energy)
  • Magnet pushed in (flux increasing): Coil creates OPPOSING pole → repels magnet (you do work → electrical energy)
  • Magnet pulled out (flux decreasing): Coil creates ATTRACTING pole → opposes withdrawal
  • Motional EMF: ε = Blv (conductor length l, speed v, perpendicular to field B)
  • Applications: Security metal detectors, tape recorders, ATM card readers, induction stoves, magnetic levitation

⚡ AC & DC Generators

  • Generator principle: Rotating coil in magnetic field → changing flux → induced EMF (Faraday's law)
  • AC Generator: Coil ends connected to SLIP RINGS → current reverses direction every half rotation → sinusoidal AC
  • DC Generator: Coil ends connected to SPLIT RINGS (commutator) → current always flows same direction → DC
  • BOTH generators: MECHANICAL energy → ELECTRICAL energy
  • Magnetic Levitation: AC solenoid → changing field → induced current in metal ring → Lenz's law → ring levitates
  • DC levitation: Ring rises briefly when current switched on/off (only during flux change), falls immediately after

📦 Minerals, Ores & Gangue

  • Mineral: Element/compound of metal occurring naturally in Earth's crust with impurities
  • Ore: Mineral from which metal can be extracted ECONOMICALLY and conveniently
  • Key fact: All ores are minerals BUT all minerals are NOT ores (e.g., Al is in many minerals but only bauxite is economical)
  • Gangue: Impurities (soil, sand, clay, rock) present in the ore
  • Flux: Substance added to ore to remove gangue by reacting with it
  • Acidic gangue (SiO₂) → Basic flux (CaO); Basic gangue (FeO) → Acidic flux (SiO₂)
  • Slag: Gangue + Flux reaction product (removed as waste). CaO + SiO₂ → CaSiO₃
  • Chalcogen family (Group 16): Most ores are OXIDES and SULPHIDES — hence the name (chalco=ore, genus=produce)

📋 Important Ores

  • Bauxite: Al₂O₃·2H₂O → Aluminium (most abundant metal in crust)
  • Haematite: Fe₂O₃ → Iron | Magnetite: Fe₃O₄ → Iron
  • Galena: PbS → Lead | Cinnabar: HgS → Mercury
  • Zinc Blende: ZnS → Zinc | Zincite: ZnO → Zinc
  • Copper Iron Pyrites: CuFeS₂ → Copper
  • Rock Salt: NaCl → Sodium | Magnesite: MgCO₃ → Magnesium
  • Limestone: CaCO₃ → Calcium | Horn Silver: AgCl → Silver
  • Reactivity: K,Na,Ca,Mg,Al (High) > Zn,Fe,Pb,Cu (Moderate) > Hg,Ag,Pt,Au (Low)

⚗️ Concentration/Dressing of Ore

  • Hand picking: Ore and impurity differ in colour/size → picked manually
  • Washing: Crushed ore on sloped surface washed → less dense impurities carried by water, dense ore stays
  • Froth Flotation: For SULPHIDE ores. Ore powder + water + pine oil + compressed air → froth carries ore to surface, gangue sinks at bottom
  • Magnetic Separation: When ore OR impurity is magnetic. Magnetic wheel attracts magnetic part
  • Purpose of concentration: Remove gangue to get enriched ore before extraction

🔥 Extraction of Crude Metal

  • HIGH REACTIVITY (K,Na,Ca,Mg,Al): Cannot use C or CO reduction (too high energy). Use ELECTROLYSIS of fused (molten) compounds
  • Na from fused NaCl: Cathode: 2Na⁺+2e⁻→2Na; Anode: 2Cl⁻→Cl₂+2e⁻
  • MODERATE REACTIVITY (Zn,Fe,Pb,Cu): Sulphide/carbonate ores → first convert to OXIDE, then reduce
  • ROASTING (heating in excess air): 2ZnS + 3O₂ → 2ZnO + 2SO₂ | 2PbS + 3O₂ → 2PbO + 2SO₂
  • CALCINATION (heating without air): MgCO₃ → MgO + CO₂ | CaCO₃ → CaO + CO₂
  • Reduction with Coke: PbO + C → Pb + CO (at 1400°C)
  • Reduction with CO: Fe₂O₃ + 3CO → 2Fe + 3CO₂ (in blast furnace)
  • THERMITE: Fe₂O₃ + 2Al → Al₂O₃ + 2Fe + Heat (welding railway tracks!)
  • AUTO REDUCTION: 2Cu₂S+3O₂→2Cu₂O+2SO₂; then 2Cu₂O+Cu₂S→6Cu+SO₂
  • LOW REACTIVITY (Hg,Ag,Au): Often found FREE in nature. Heat or displacement. 2HgS+3O₂→2HgO→2Hg+O₂

✨ Refining of Metals

  • Distillation: For low boiling metals (Zn, Hg). Impure metal distilled → pure metal as distillate
  • Poling: Molten metal stirred with GREEN WOOD logs. Reducing gases prevent Cu oxidation. Impurities oxidised → slag. Used for blister copper
  • Liquation: Low-melting metal (Sn/tin) flows down sloped surface, high-melting impurities left behind
  • Electrolytic Refining: IMPURE metal = ANODE; PURE metal strip = CATHODE; Metal salt solution = ELECTROLYTE
  • Anode dissolves: M → Mⁿ⁺ + ne⁻; Pure metal deposits on cathode: Mⁿ⁺ + ne⁻ → M
  • Anode mud: Insoluble impurities at anode bottom (may contain Au, Ag, Pt, Se, Te → valuable!)
  • Copper is refined this way using CuSO₄ solution as electrolyte

🛡️ Corrosion & Prevention

  • Iron rusts ONLY in presence of BOTH water AND air (oxygen) — not with either alone
  • Rusting is an ELECTROCHEMICAL process
  • Anodic spot: 2Fe → 2Fe²⁺ + 4e⁻ | Cathodic spot: O₂ + 4H⁺ + 4e⁻ → 2H₂O
  • Rust = Fe₂O₃·xH₂O (hydrated ferric oxide)
  • Prevention: Painting, chemical coating (bisphenol), electroplating with Sn or Zn
  • Sacrificial electrode: Mg or Zn attached — corrodes itself but PROTECTS the iron object
  • Alloying to prevent corrosion: Fe + Ni + Cr = Stainless steel (does NOT rust!)
  • Pure gold (24 carat) is too soft → alloyed with Ag or Cu. India uses 22 carat gold (22 parts Au + 2 parts Ag/Cu)

Carbon and its Compounds
TS SSC Class 10 · Boards 2026

🌟 Carbon's Special Properties

  • Carbon: Group 14 (IVA), Atomic number 6, Electronic config: 1s²2s²2p². Has 4 VALENCE ELECTRONS → Tetravalency
  • Cannot form C⁴⁻ (6 protons can't hold 10 electrons) or C⁴⁺ (needs too much energy) → forms COVALENT bonds
  • Tetravalency: 4 single bonds, or 1 double + 2 single, or 1 triple + 1 single, or 2 double bonds
  • Catenation: Carbon forms LONGEST chains with its OWN atoms (millions of C atoms in proteins). S, P show it but much less
  • Carbon is VERSATILE: Largest number of compounds due to tetravalency + catenation + multiple bond types
  • Hybridisation concept by Linus Pauling (1931): Redistribution of equal-energy orbitals → new identical hybrid orbitals

🔗 Hybridisation Types

  • sp³ (Diamond, Methane, Ethane): 1s + 3p → 4 sp³ hybrid orbitals. Shape: TETRAHEDRAL. Bond angle = 109°28'. Only sigma bonds
  • sp² (Graphite, Ethene): 1s + 2p → 3 sp² orbitals + 1 unhybridised p. Shape: TRIGONAL PLANAR. Bond angle = 120°
  • Ethene C₂H₄: sp²-sp² sigma bond + pz-pz π bond = DOUBLE BOND (1σ + 1π). Bond angle H-C-H = 120°
  • sp (Ethyne/Acetylene): 1s + 1p → 2 sp orbitals + 2 unhybridised p orbitals. Shape: LINEAR. Bond angle = 180°
  • Ethyne C₂H₂: sp-sp sigma bond + two π bonds (py-py and pz-pz) = TRIPLE BOND (1σ + 2π)
  • Bond strength order: C≡C > C=C > C-C | Bond length order: C≡C < C=C < C-C

💎 Allotropes of Carbon

  • Allotropy: Element existing in 2+ physical forms with different physical properties but same chemical properties
  • Diamond (sp³): 3D tetrahedral network. Very strong C-C bonds → HARDEST natural substance. Does NOT conduct electricity (no free electrons)
  • Graphite (sp²): 2D layered structure. Layers separated by 3.35 Å. GOOD CONDUCTOR due to delocalised π electrons. Lubricant, pencil lead
  • When writing with pencil: Interlayer attractions break → graphite layers deposit on paper → easily erased
  • Buckminsterfullerene C₆₀ (sp²): Soccer ball shape. 12 pentagonal + 20 hexagonal faces. Discovered 1985 by Curl, Kroto & Smalley → Nobel Prize 1996
  • Nanotubes: Graphite sheets rolled into cylinders. Discovered 1991 by Sumio Iijima. Good electrical conductors like graphite
  • Graphene: Single layer of graphite (hexagonal honeycomb). 200× stronger than steel, conducts electricity better than copper
  • Amorphous forms: Coal, Coke, Wood charcoal, Animal charcoal, Lamp black, Gas carbon

🔗 Hydrocarbons & Classification

  • Hydrocarbons: Compounds containing ONLY C and H. Two types: Open chain (aliphatic) and Closed chain (cyclic)
  • Alkanes (CₙH₂ₙ₊₂): Only C-C single bonds. SATURATED. Chemically least reactive → called PARAFFINS (parum=little affinity)
  • Alkenes (CₙH₂ₙ): At least one C=C. UNSATURATED. More reactive than alkanes
  • Alkynes (CₙH₂ₙ₋₂): At least one C≡C. UNSATURATED. Most reactive of the three
  • Homologous series: Each successive compound differs by -CH₂. Same general formula, same functional group, similar chemical properties
  • Organic compounds defined as 'compounds of carbon'. Berzelius coined organic/inorganic. Wöhler synthesised urea from NH₄CNO (1828) → disproved vital force theory
  • Isomerism: Same molecular formula but different structural formulae. E.g., n-butane and isobutane (both C₄H₁₀)

📝 IUPAC Nomenclature

  • Word root: C1=meth, C2=eth, C3=prop, C4=but, C5=pent, C6=hex, C7=hept, C8=oct, C9=non, C10=dec
  • Primary suffix: Single bond = -ane | Double bond = -ene | Triple bond = -yne
  • Secondary suffix: Alcohol = -ol | Aldehyde = -al | Ketone = -one | Carboxylic acid = -oic acid | Amine = -amine
  • Numbering rule: Lowest sum rule — number from end giving LOWEST numbers to substituents + functional groups
  • Priority of principal group: -COOH > -CHO > C=O > -OH > -NH₂
  • Examples: CH₃OH = Methanol | CH₃CH₂OH = Ethanol | CH₃COOH = Ethanoic acid | CH₃CHO = Ethanal
  • Cyclic compounds get prefix 'cyclo': CH₂CH₂CH₂ (ring) = Cyclopropane

⚗️ Chemical Reactions of Carbon Compounds

  • 1. COMBUSTION: C compounds + O₂ → CO₂ + H₂O + Energy. Saturated = clear BLUE flame. Unsaturated = YELLOW sooty flame
  • 2. OXIDATION: Ethanol → Ethanal → Ethanoic acid (using alkaline KMnO₄ or acidified K₂Cr₂O₇)
  • CH₃CH₂OH → CH₃CHO → CH₃COOH (Ethanol → Acetaldehyde → Acetic acid)
  • Police breathalyser uses K₂Cr₂O₇: Orange Cr₂O₇²⁻ → bluish green Cr³⁺ when ethanol is oxidised
  • 3. ADDITION: Unsaturated (alkenes/alkynes) + H₂ → Saturated. Catalyst: NICKEL. Used in vegetable ghee production
  • 4. SUBSTITUTION: Saturated alkanes + Cl₂ (in sunlight): CH₄+Cl₂→CH₃Cl→CH₂Cl₂→CHCl₃→CCl₄

🧪 Important Carbon Compounds

  • Ethanol (C₂H₅OH): Colourless liquid, bp 78.3°C. Prepared by: Fermentation of sugars/starch with yeast, OR addition of H₂O to ethene
  • Ethanol + Na: 2C₂H₅OH + 2Na → 2C₂H₅ONa (sodium ethoxide) + H₂ (hydrogen)
  • Ethanol + conc. H₂SO₄ (170°C): Dehydration → CH₂=CH₂ (ethene). H₂SO₄ = dehydrating agent
  • Denatured alcohol: Ethanol + methanol (toxic) → undrinkable. 200 mL methanol = fatal dose
  • Ethanoic acid (CH₃COOH): Colourless, unpleasant odour. 5-8% in water = VINEGAR (used as preservative in pickles)
  • Esterification: RCOOH + R'OH ⇌ RCOOR' + H₂O (conc. H₂SO₄ catalyst). Reaction is SLOW and REVERSIBLE
  • Example: CH₃COOH + C₂H₅OH ⇌ CH₃COOC₂H₅ (ethyl acetate, sweet smell) + H₂O
  • Saponification: Fat (ester) + NaOH → RCOONa (soap) + Glycerol
  • Soap = Na/K salt of higher fatty acid. Micelle: Hydrophilic end (COO⁻ Na⁺) faces water; Hydrophobic end (hydrocarbon) traps grease/dirt
